Concept 01 — Orbital Seal

Primary mark, standalone
The Orbital Seal
Astrolabe · observatory instrument · scientific society emblem

This is the most literal read of “looking down on the solar system as a coordinate diagram.” The outermost bezel carries 32 fine graduated ticks — one per combined climate period in the underlying system, at true watch-grade density — framed by its own thin double ring. Just inside it, the secondary ring now carries 8 solid gold dots at true 45° spacing, marking the 8 Season Points as the earth travels its orbit around the sun — read as 8 fixed waypoints along the ecliptic rather than tick-mark gradations. Inside that, an 8-point compass-rose star radiates from Earth at center: its 4 long spokes reach exactly to the 4 Lunar Phase glyphs sitting on the inner ring (hollow, half-lit, full, half-lit), while 4 shorter spokes mark the in-between season directions — so the star is doing double duty as both compass rose and the literal geometry connecting Earth to each lunar phase.

Why it works as a seal: the triple-ring-plus-radiating-star structure is exactly the grammar of a scientific instrument plate or a university roundel — it doesn’t copy any one of them, but every element (graduated bezel, dotted orbital ring, spokes fixed to a center point) belongs to that family. It also happens to already contain a very legible “O” — the whole seal is the letter.

Where it's strongest: primary logo, letterhead, wax-seal-style stamp, embroidered patch. Where it strains: the 32-tick bezel and lunar glyphs get delicate below ~32px, so at true favicon size they simplify to plain rings (see the icon variant below).
