The Ecliptic Cycle

Aspect A · 8 Season Points · One Solar Year

Earth moves counterclockwise around the Sun over the course of one year. Each Season Point is one of eight equally-spaced positions in that orbit, measured in degrees of ecliptic longitude. The equinoxes (Breaking & Reckoning) and solstices (Cresting & Kindling) anchor the four quarters; the cross-quarter points (Sprouting, Reaping, Veiling, Rooting) fall at the true astronomical midpoints between them.
